Origin Field Theory (OFT) is a speculative theoretical framework that investigates whether law selection can be modeled as a stability-weighted conditioning process over a space of possible physical and informational configurations.

The original OFT proposal framed existence as arising from an atemporal relation between an Origin Field, understood as a measure space of possible configurations, and a Focus relation that conditions this space into stable, law-governed structures. In that formulation, time is not treated as fundamental; instead, temporal order appears as an emergent index of informational stabilization. The theory also introduced observer capacity as one term in a broader stability functional, alongside self-consistency, algorithmic simplicity, and fragility.

The updated version narrows the claim substantially. Rather than presenting OFT as a confirmed theory of consciousness, cosmology, or physical law selection, the current work treats it as a research program for constructing and auditing stability-weighted priors over finite model ensembles.

In this updated form, OFT asks a more limited and testable question:

Can a declared stability functional be used to construct a reproducible prior over a finite ensemble of possible laws or rule systems?

The v1.5.0 implementation explores this question using elementary cellular automata as a toy universe ensemble. The complete 256-rule ECA space is used as a finite, fully enumerable testbed. Each rule is evaluated using explicit proxy measures for stability-related properties, and those scores are converted into a Gibbs-style prior distribution. The result is not a claim that cellular automata are physical laws, but a controlled demonstration of how a stability-based law-selection framework can be made explicit, reproducible, and auditable.

The updated framework emphasizes:

- finite declared model ensembles,
- explicit scoring assumptions,
- reproducible prior construction,
- robustness analysis,
- null-model comparison,
- uncertainty intervals,
- finite-size diagnostics,
- manifest validation,
- and clear separation between methodological results and physical claims.

The central supported claim is methodological: stability-weighted prior construction can be formalized, implemented, and audited over a finite ensemble.

The current release does not claim to prove the full Origin Field ontology, establish consciousness as a fundamental physical operator, replace ΛCDM, modify quantum mechanics, or confirm a new mechanism of cosmological law selection. Those broader claims remain speculative. The value of the updated version is that it converts one part of the original OFT idea into a constrained research artifact that can be tested, criticized, reproduced, and extended.

In short, the updated Origin Field Theory should be understood as a bridge between speculative law-selection philosophy and reproducible model-selection methodology. It preserves the original motivation — explaining why stable law-like structures might be favored — while replacing unsupported certainty with finite-ensemble demonstrations, explicit assumptions, and falsifiable research pathways.
